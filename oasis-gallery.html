Amazone Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest spans nine countries in South America, with the largest portion located in Brazil. It extends into Peru, Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, Bolivia, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana, covering a vast and diverse region known for its lush biodiversity.

The Grand Canyon

pThe Grand Canyon is located in the southwestern United States, primarily in the state of Arizona. It's carved by the Colorado River and is one of the most iconic and breathtaking natural wonders on Earth.

Yellowstone

Yellowstone National Park is situated primarily in the U.S. states of Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho. It was the world's first national park and is renowned for its geothermal wonders, including geysers like Old Faithful, as well as its stunning landscapes and wildlife.

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is located on the border between the United States and Canada. It straddles the state of New York in the USA and the province of Ontario in Canada, making it one of the most famous natural attractions in North America.

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is situated on the Honshu Island of Japan. It is the country's highest peak and an iconic symbol of Japan, attracting hikers and tourists from around the world. Mount Fuji is part of the Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park.

Hanging Lake

Hanging Lake is located in Colorado, USA, near the town of Glenwood Springs. It's a unique and picturesque lake known for its crystal-clear waters and the lush hanging gardens of plants and moss that surround it.
